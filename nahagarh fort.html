Nahargarh Fort

About Nahargarh Fort

Nahargarh Fort is a historic fort overlooking the pink city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it was originally named Sudarshangarh Fort. The fort was later renamed Nahargarh, which means 'abode of tigers'.

Nahargarh Fort, along with Amer Fort and Jaigarh Fort, forms the defensive ring around the city of Jaipur. It offers panoramic views of the city and is a popular tourist attraction known for its architectural beauty and historical significance.

Main Points

  • Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II.
  • Originally named Sudarshangarh Fort, later renamed Nahargarh Fort.
  • Forms part of the defensive ring along with Amer Fort and Jaigarh Fort.
  • Offers stunning views of Jaipur city and surrounding landscapes.
  • Houses several palaces, temples, and structures within its complex.

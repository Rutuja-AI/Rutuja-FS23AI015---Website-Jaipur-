Jal Mahal

About Jal Mahal

Jal Mahal, or the Water Palace, is an architectural marvel located in the middle of the Man Sagar Lake in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. It is a five-story palace, with four floors submerged in water when the lake is full, leaving only the top floor visible.

Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Madho Singh I, Jal Mahal is renowned for its stunning Rajput and Mughal architectural styles. The palace offers breathtaking views against the backdrop of the Aravalli Hills and is a popular spot for tourists and photographers.

Main Points

  • Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Madho Singh I.
  • Constructed in the Rajput and Mughal architectural styles.
  • Located in the middle of the Man Sagar Lake.
  • Consists of five floors, with four submerged in water.
  • Offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

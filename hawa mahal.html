Hawa Mahal

About Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a prominent landmark in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the palace is renowned for its intricate latticework windows (Jharokhas) that allow cool air to circulate, giving it the name "Hawa Mahal."

The five-story palace, constructed of red and pink sandstone, is shaped like a crown or a honeycomb and is adorned with over 900 small windows. These windows not only provide ventilation but also offer stunning views of the city.

Main Points

  • Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.
  • Designed by Lal Chand Ustad in the form of the crown of Krishna.
  • Features a unique architectural style with intricate latticework windows.
  • Constructed for the royal women to observe street festivals without being seen.
  • Offers panoramic views of Jaipur city from the top floors.

City Palace Jaipur

About City Palace

The City Palace, Jaipur, was established by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, and is a symbol of the royal heritage of the city. It is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Jaipur and showcases a fine blend of Rajput, Mughal, and European architectural styles.

The palace complex comprises several courtyards, gardens, and buildings, including the Chandra Mahal, Mubarak Mahal, and Diwan-i-Khas. It also houses the Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II Museum, which exhibits an extensive collection of royal artifacts, costumes, and weapons.

Main Points

  • Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II.
  • Combines Rajput, Mughal, and European architectural elements.
  • Home to the royal family of Jaipur and the Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II Museum.
  • Offers guided tours to explore the palace complex and its rich history.
  • Located in the heart of the Pink City, adjacent to the Hawa Mahal.
